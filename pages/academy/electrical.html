Electrical Engineering

Power systems, protection coordination, load calculation. Includes switchgear, MCC, distribution panels, and lighting systems. Essential for all industrial and commercial projects.

Overview

Electrical engineering is the core of modern infrastructure. It covers everything from power generation to end-user consumption. Key areas include:

  • Power Generation (Thermal, Hydro, Solar, Wind)
  • Transmission and Distribution Networks
  • Protection and Control Systems
  • Industrial and Commercial Installations
📌 Key Standard: IEC 60364 - Electrical Installations

Power System Analysis

Power system analysis involves studying the behavior of electrical networks under normal and fault conditions.

  • Load Flow Analysis
  • Short-Circuit Analysis
  • Stability Studies
  • Harmonic Analysis
📌 Software: ETAP, DIgSILENT, SKM

Protection Coordination

Protection coordination ensures that the correct protective devices operate to isolate faults without affecting the rest of the system.

  • Overcurrent Protection
  • Earth Fault Protection
  • Differential Protection
  • Circuit Breaker Coordination
📌 Standard: IEC 60947 - Low-voltage switchgear

Switchgear & MCC

Switchgear and MCCs are the heart of power distribution and motor control in industrial plants.

  • LV and MV Switchgear
  • Motor Control Centers (MCC)
  • Distribution Panels
  • Busbar Systems
📌 Standard: IEC 61439 - Switchgear assemblies

Load Calculation

Load calculation is essential for sizing cables, breakers, transformers, and other electrical equipment.

  • Demand Factor and Diversity Factor
  • Cable Sizing
  • Transformer Sizing
  • Voltage Drop Calculation
📌 Formula: P = √3 × V × I × PF

Lighting Design

Lighting design involves selecting luminaires and calculating illumination levels for industrial and commercial spaces.

  • Lux Level Calculation
  • Luminaire Selection
  • Energy Efficiency
  • Emergency Lighting
📌 Software: DIALux, Relux

Earthing Systems

Earthing systems protect equipment and personnel by providing a low-impedance path to ground.

  • Earth Electrode Systems
  • Bonding and Equipotential Bonding
  • Earth Resistance Measurement
  • Lightning Protection
📌 Standard: IEEE 80 - Earthing Systems
