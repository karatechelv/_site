Designing cable routing systems including cable trays, conduits, cable schedules, and terminations.

What is Cable Routing?

Cable routing is the process of planning and designing pathways for cables in an industrial or building environment. It involves selecting cable trays, conduits, raceways, and ensuring proper segregation of power, control, and communication cables.

Cable Routing Components

  • Cable Trays – Ladder, perforated, or solid bottom
  • Conduits – Rigid, flexible, PVC, or metallic
  • Raceways – For indoor distribution
  • Cable Ladders – Heavy-duty cable support
  • Glands & Cable Entries – For panels and enclosures
  • Cable Ties & Supports – Securing and organizing

Cable Segregation

  • Power Cables (High Voltage) – Must be segregated from low voltage
  • Control Cables (PLC, Instrument) – Avoid electrical noise
  • Communication Cables (Ethernet, Fiber) – Must be protected
  • Separation distance (minimum 300mm for power vs control)
