Cable Routing & Sizing

Designing cable routing systems, cable trays, conduits, and cable sizing for electrical systems.

What is Cable Routing?

Cable routing is the design of pathways for electrical cables from power sources to loads. It includes cable trays, conduits, ladder racks, and direct burial methods. Proper routing ensures safety, reduces electromagnetic interference, and simplifies maintenance.

Cable Routing Methods

  • Cable Trays – Perforated, solid-bottom, ladder-type
  • Conduits – PVC, metal (rigid/EMT), flexible
  • Trunking & Ducts – For control panels and buildings
  • Direct Burial – Underground cables with protection
  • Overhead – Cable ladders and supports

Cable Sizing

  • Current Carrying Capacity – Based on ampacity tables
  • Voltage Drop – Must be within allowable limits
  • Short-Circuit Rating – Thermal and mechanical withstand
  • Derating Factors – Temperature, grouping, ambient conditions

Cable sizing follows IEC 60364 or NEC (NFPA 70) standards.

Cable Tray Loading

  • Calculate total cable weight per meter
  • Select tray width and depth based on fill capacity
  • Consider spacing for heat dissipation
  • Apply safety factors for future expansion

Example: Cable Schedule

A cable schedule is a document listing all cables used in a project. It includes cable number, type, size, length, source, destination, and remarks. This is essential for procurement and installation planning.
