Panel Layout Design

Designing electrical and control panels, component placement, enclosure selection, and busbar sizing.

What is Panel Layout?

Panel layout is the physical arrangement of electrical and electronic components inside an enclosure. It includes placement of circuit breakers, contactors, PLCs, terminal blocks, power supplies, and wiring ducts. A good layout ensures safety, accessibility, and thermal management.

Key Components

  • Main Circuit Breaker & Distribution Busbars
  • PLC Rack & I/O Modules
  • Power Supplies (24VDC, 48VDC)
  • Contactors, Relays, & Overloads
  • Terminal Blocks & Wiring Ducts
  • HMI/Operator Interface

Design Considerations

  • Heat Dissipation – Ventilation and cooling
  • Clearance – Spacing for safety and maintenance
  • Cable Entry – Glands, cable trays, and routing
  • Short-Circuit Withstand – Busbar and component ratings
  • Environmental Protection – IP ratings, ingress protection

Example: MCC Panel Layout

A Motor Control Center (MCC) panel typically includes a main incoming breaker, busbars, multiple motor starters (DOL, Star-Delta), PLC I/O modules, and a communication gateway. The layout is divided into sections for incoming power, distribution, and control.
