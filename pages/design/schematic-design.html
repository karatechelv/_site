Electrical Schematics

Creating and interpreting electrical schematics, one-line diagrams, three-line diagrams, and wiring diagrams.

What are Electrical Schematics?

Electrical schematics are graphical representations of electrical circuits and systems. They use standardized symbols to show components, connections, and power flow. They are essential for design, installation, and troubleshooting.

Types of Schematics

  • One-Line Diagrams – Simplified power system representation
  • Three-Line Diagrams – Detailed power and control wiring
  • Wiring Diagrams – Physical connection details
  • Block Diagrams – System-level functional representation
  • Interconnection Diagrams – Equipment-to-equipment connections

Common Symbols

  • Resistors, Capacitors, Inductors
  • Switches, Relays, Contactors
  • Motors, Generators, Transformers
  • Fuses, Circuit Breakers, Overloads
  • PLC I/O, Instruments, Sensors

Example: Motor Control Schematic

A typical motor control schematic includes: Power supply → Circuit breaker → Contactor → Overload relay → Motor. Control circuit includes Start/Stop buttons, auxiliary contacts, and interlocking. This forms the basis of most industrial motor control designs.
