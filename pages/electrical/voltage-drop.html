What is Voltage Drop?

Voltage drop is the reduction in voltage in an electrical circuit between the source and the load. It occurs due to the resistance of the conductors and is influenced by the cable length, cross-sectional area, and the amount of current flowing.

Excessive voltage drop can cause equipment malfunction, reduced efficiency, and overheating of cables. Therefore, it is important to keep voltage drop within acceptable limits (typically less than 3-5% of the nominal voltage).

Key Factors Affecting Voltage Drop

  • Conductor Material: Copper vs. Aluminum
  • Cable Length: Longer cables have higher voltage drop
  • Cross-Sectional Area: Larger area reduces voltage drop
  • Current Load: Higher current increases voltage drop
