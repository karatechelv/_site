Protection Coordination

A comprehensive guide to protection coordination, including protective devices, relay settings, and time-current curves.

What is Protection Coordination?

Protection coordination ensures that the correct protective devices operate to isolate faults without affecting the rest of the system. It is critical for system reliability and safety.

This involves selecting and setting protective relays, circuit breakers, and fuses to achieve selective tripping. Time-current curves and coordination studies are used to achieve optimal protection.

Protective Devices

  • Circuit Breakers - LV, MV, and HV breakers
  • Fuses - Protection against overcurrent
  • Protective Relays - Overcurrent, earth fault, differential, distance
  • Motor Protection - Thermal overload, short-circuit

Time-Current Curves

Time-current curves show the relationship between the magnitude of the fault current and the operating time of protective devices. They are used to coordinate devices and ensure selective tripping.

  • Inverse Time
  • Definite Time
  • Very Inverse
  • Extremely Inverse
