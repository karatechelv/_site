📊 Power Factor

Understanding power factor, its importance, and how to improve it in electrical systems.

What is Power Factor?

Power factor is the ratio of real power to apparent power in an AC circuit. It ranges from 0 to 1, with 1 being ideal. A low power factor indicates inefficient use of electrical power.

Why is it Important?

Low power factor results in higher current for the same load, leading to increased losses, higher electricity bills, and reduced system capacity.

How to Improve Power Factor

Power factor correction can be achieved using capacitor banks, synchronous condensers, or active power factor correction devices.
