What is Power Factor?

Power factor is the ratio of real power (kW) to apparent power (kVA). It indicates how effectively electrical power is being used. A power factor of 1 (unity) means all the power is being used efficiently, while a lower power factor indicates inefficiency.

Improving power factor reduces energy losses, lowers electricity bills, and increases system capacity. Common methods include using capacitors and synchronous condensers.
