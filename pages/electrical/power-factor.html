📊 Power Factor

Calculate power factor, apparent power, and reactive power. Essential for power system analysis.

What is Power Factor?

Power factor is the ratio of real power (kW) to apparent power (kVA). It indicates how effectively electrical power is being used. A power factor of 1 (unity) means all the power is being used efficiently.

Key Formulas

PF = P / S
Q = √(S² - P²)
  • P = Real Power (kW)
  • S = Apparent Power (kVA)
  • Q = Reactive Power (kVAr)
  • PF = Power Factor

Power Factor Correction

Improving power factor reduces energy losses, lowers electricity bills, and increases system capacity. Common methods include using capacitors and synchronous condensers.
