Power System Analysis

Complete guide to power system analysis including load flow, short-circuit, stability, and harmonic studies.

Introduction

Power system analysis is the study of electrical networks to ensure reliable and efficient operation. It involves mathematical modeling and simulation of power systems under various conditions.

The main objectives are: system planning, operation optimization, fault analysis, and ensuring stability and security of the power grid.

Load Flow Analysis

Load flow analysis (also called power flow) determines the voltage, current, and power flow in a power system under steady-state conditions.

It is used for: system planning, voltage profile analysis, loss calculation, and contingency analysis.

  • Newton-Raphson Method
  • Gauss-Seidel Method
  • Fast Decoupled Method
📌 Software: ETAP, DIgSILENT, SKM

Short-Circuit Analysis

Short-circuit analysis calculates fault currents and determines the required ratings of protective devices such as circuit breakers and fuses.

  • Three-phase fault
  • Line-to-ground fault
  • Line-to-line fault
  • Double line-to-ground fault
📌 Standard: IEC 60909

Stability Studies

Stability studies evaluate the ability of a power system to maintain synchronism after a disturbance such as a fault or load change.

  • Transient Stability
  • Steady-State Stability
  • Voltage Stability
  • Frequency Stability

Harmonic Analysis

Harmonic analysis identifies and mitigates harmonic distortion caused by non-linear loads such as VFDs, UPS, and rectifiers.

  • Total Harmonic Distortion (THD)
  • Individual Harmonics
  • Harmonic Filters
📌 Standard: IEEE 519

Summary

Power system analysis is a critical tool for engineers to ensure the reliability, safety, and efficiency of electrical power systems. Proper analysis leads to better system design, operation, and protection.
