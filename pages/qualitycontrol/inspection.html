Inspection in Quality Control

Inspection methods, techniques, and procedures for quality control in engineering projects.

What is Inspection?

Inspection is the process of examining materials, components, or finished products to determine conformity with specified requirements. It involves visual checks, measurements, and testing to identify defects or deviations from standards.

Types of Inspection

  • Visual Inspection – Surface defects, cracks, corrosion
  • Dimensional Inspection – Measurements using gauges and CMM
  • Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) – Ultrasonic, MPI, Dye Penetrant
  • Destructive Testing – Mechanical, tensile, impact testing
  • Electrical Testing – Continuity, insulation resistance, hi-pot

Inspection Stages

  • Raw Material Inspection – Before production starts
  • In-Process Inspection – During fabrication/assembly
  • Final Inspection – Before dispatch to customer
  • Source Inspection – At supplier's facility

Inspection Tools & Equipment

  • Calipers, Micrometers, Gauges
  • Coordinate Measuring Machines (CMM)
  • Ultrasonic Flaw Detectors
  • Magnetic Particle Inspection (MPI) Equipment
  • Dye Penetrant Kits
  • Insulation Testers / Meggers

Example: Welding Inspection

A welding inspection includes visual examination of weld appearance, dimensional checks, and non-destructive testing (ultrasonic or radiographic). The inspection ensures the weld meets code requirements (e.g., ASME Section IX) and is free from defects like cracks, porosity, or lack of fusion.
