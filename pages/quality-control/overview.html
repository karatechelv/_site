Overview of Quality Control

A comprehensive overview of quality control engineering, covering inspection, testing, quality plans, and continuous improvement.

What is Quality Control?

Quality Control (QC) is the process of ensuring that products and services meet specified quality requirements. It involves inspection, testing, and monitoring of processes to detect and correct defects, ensuring customer satisfaction and regulatory compliance.

Key Quality Control Areas

  • Incoming Material Inspection – Raw materials and components
  • In-Process Inspection – During manufacturing/assembly
  • Final Inspection – Before delivery to customer
  • Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) – Ultrasonic, radiography, dye penetrant
  • Destructive Testing – Mechanical, electrical, environmental testing

Quality Management Principles

  • Customer Focus – Meeting customer requirements
  • Leadership – Clear quality vision and direction
  • Engagement of People – Involving all employees
  • Process Approach – Managing activities as processes
  • Continual Improvement – Ongoing enhancement of performance
