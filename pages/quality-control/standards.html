Quality Standards

International quality standards including ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and industry-specific quality requirements.

What are Quality Standards?

Quality standards are documented guidelines, specifications, and requirements that ensure products, services, and processes meet consistent quality levels. They provide a framework for quality management and continuous improvement.

ISO Quality Standards

  • ISO 9001 – Quality Management Systems (QMS)
  • ISO 14001 – Environmental Management Systems
  • ISO 45001 – Occupational Health & Safety
  • ISO 50001 – Energy Management
  • ISO 17025 – Testing and Calibration Laboratories

Industry-Specific Quality Standards

  • IATF 16949 – Automotive Quality Management
  • AS9100 – Aerospace Quality Management
  • API Q1 – Oil & Gas Quality Management
  • ISO 13485 – Medical Device Quality Management
  • FSSC 22000 – Food Safety Management

Quality Tools & Methods

  • PDCA Cycle – Plan-Do-Check-Act
  • 8D Problem Solving – Eight Disciplines
  • Fishbone Diagram – Root cause analysis
  • Pareto Analysis – Prioritizing improvements
  • 5 Whys – Finding root causes
  • FMEA – Failure Mode and Effects Analysis

Example: ISO 9001 Implementation

Implementing ISO 9001 involves defining quality policy, documenting procedures, training employees, conducting internal audits, and continuous improvement. Certification is achieved through an external audit by an accredited body.
