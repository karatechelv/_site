Quality Plans

Creating and managing quality plans, Inspection and Test Plans (ITP), and quality control procedures.

What is a Quality Plan?

A Quality Plan is a document that specifies the quality standards, practices, resources, and activities relevant to a particular project or product. It defines how quality will be managed and ensured throughout the project lifecycle.

Key Elements of a Quality Plan

  • Quality Objectives – Measurable goals
  • Scope – What is covered by the plan
  • Roles & Responsibilities – Who does what
  • Quality Standards – Applicable standards and specifications
  • Inspection & Test Plan (ITP) – What, when, and how to inspect/test
  • Documentation – Records and reporting requirements

Inspection & Test Plan (ITP)

  • Activity – What is being inspected/tested
  • Acceptance Criteria – Pass/fail criteria
  • Method – How it will be inspected/tested
  • Frequency – How often
  • Responsible Person – Who performs the inspection
  • Documentation – What records are kept

ITP Example for Control Panel

# ITP for Control Panel (MCC-01) # ------------------------------------------------- # Activity | Method | Criteria | Record # ------------------------------------------------- # Visual Inspection | Visual check | No damage | Check Sheet # Wiring Check | Continuity | All connected | Continuity Log # Insulation Test | Megger | > 1 MΩ | Test Report # Power-up Test | Multimeter | 24VDC ±5% | Test Report # Function Test | Simulation | All I/O works | Function Log # Communication | Ping test | Online | Comm Report

Example: Quality Plan for Panel Project

A quality plan for a panel manufacturing project includes: project scope, quality objectives (zero defects, 100% test coverage), approved supplier list, inspection and test plan (ITP) with hold points, documentation requirements (test reports, NCRs), and final acceptance criteria.
