🔬 Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)

Non-destructive testing methods for inspecting materials and components without causing damage.

🔹 What is NDT?

Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) is a method of inspecting materials, components, and assemblies for defects without causing damage. It is used to ensure the integrity and reliability of products and structures across various industries.

🔹 Common NDT Methods

  • Ultrasonic Testing (UT) – High-frequency sound waves
  • Radiographic Testing (RT) – X-rays or gamma rays
  • Magnetic Particle Inspection (MPI) – Magnetic fields
  • Dye Penetrant Testing (PT) – Capillary action
  • Eddy Current Testing (ET) – Electromagnetic induction
  • Visual Testing (VT) – Direct observation

🔹 Applications

  • Weld inspection
  • Castings and forgings
  • Pipeline inspection
  • Aerospace components
  • Structural integrity assessment
