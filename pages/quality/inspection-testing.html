🔍 Inspection & Testing

Methods and procedures for inspection and testing in quality control, including incoming inspection, in-process control, and final testing.

🔹 What is Inspection & Testing?

Inspection and testing are critical quality control activities that verify products and processes meet specifications. Inspection involves examining products for defects, while testing measures performance against requirements. Both are essential for ensuring product quality, safety, and compliance.

🔹 Types of Inspection

  • Incoming Inspection – Verifying raw materials and components before production
  • In-Process Inspection – Monitoring production processes at key stages
  • Final Inspection – Checking finished products before release
  • First Article Inspection (FAI) – Validating new production runs
  • Destructive vs. Non-destructive Testing – NDT (X-ray, ultrasonic) vs. destructive (tensile, impact)

🔹 Common Test Methods

  • Visual Inspection – Checking for surface defects, dimensions
  • Dimensional Measurement – Using calipers, micrometers, CMM
  • Electrical Testing – Continuity, insulation resistance, hi-pot
  • Mechanical Testing – Tensile, hardness, impact, fatigue
  • Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) – Radiography, ultrasonic, dye penetrant
  • Functional Testing – Testing product operation under real conditions

🔹 Example: Inspection of Electrical Panels

An electrical panel manufacturer performs incoming inspection on components, in-process checks during assembly, and final functional testing (e.g., voltage checks, insulation resistance, and control logic verification). All results are documented in inspection reports.
