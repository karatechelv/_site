✅ Site Acceptance Test (SAT)

SAT is the final testing phase conducted on-site after installation to verify that the complete system operates correctly and meets all contractual and performance requirements.

🔹 What is SAT?

The Site Acceptance Test (SAT) is the final verification of the installed system on the project site. It confirms that the system functions as intended under actual operating conditions and meets all specified performance, safety, and reliability criteria. SAT is typically conducted by the contractor in the presence of the client and is a prerequisite for final acceptance and handover.

🔹 Key SAT Activities

  • Visual Inspection – Verify that installation is complete and matches as-built drawings
  • Functional Testing – Test all systems under normal and abnormal conditions
  • Performance Testing – Demonstrate that capacity, efficiency, and output meet contractual requirements
  • Safety Testing – Verify all safety systems, interlocks, and emergency procedures are effective
  • Integration Testing – Confirm seamless integration of all subsystems
  • Documentation Review – Ensure all manuals, as-built drawings, and test certificates are complete
  • Client Sign-off – Formal acceptance and handover after successful completion of all tests

🔹 SAT vs. FAT

  • FAT – Conducted at the supplier's facility before shipment. Focuses on verifying that equipment meets specifications in a controlled environment.
  • SAT – Conducted on-site after installation. Focuses on verifying that the complete system operates correctly under actual site conditions (temperature, humidity, power quality, etc.).
  • SAT is more comprehensive and includes integration with existing systems, site-specific utilities, and operator training.

🔹 Example: SAT for a Water Treatment Plant

A water treatment plant undergoes SAT after installation. The test includes running the plant at full capacity for 72 hours, measuring flow rates, chemical dosing accuracy, and effluent quality. All alarms and interlocks are tested, and the SCADA system is verified for proper data acquisition and control. After successful completion, the client signs the acceptance certificate, and the plant is handed over to operations.
