Site Acceptance Test (SAT)

Final testing and verification at the customer's site before handover.

What is SAT?

Site Acceptance Test (SAT) is the final testing phase conducted at the customer's site after installation and commissioning. It verifies that the system operates correctly under actual site conditions and meets all performance requirements.

SAT Procedure

  • System Inspection – Check installation quality
  • Functional Testing – Test all functions with actual signals
  • Performance Testing – Verify under real load
  • Safety Testing – Check all safety systems
  • Communication Testing – Verify all network links
  • Documentation – Review all test records
  • Client Witness – Customer observation and sign-off

SAT vs FAT

  • FAT – At manufacturer's facility, simulated conditions
  • SAT – At customer's site, actual conditions
  • FAT – Before shipment, to ensure quality
  • SAT – After installation, for final acceptance
  • Both are required for project completion

Example: MCC Panel SAT

The SAT for an MCC panel includes: starting and stopping all motors under load, verifying SCADA data matches field readings, testing all alarms and safety interlocks, and confirming system performance. The client signs off upon successful completion.
