Procurement Phase

Sourcing, purchasing, and managing materials and components for engineering projects.

What is Procurement?

Procurement is the process of sourcing, purchasing, and managing materials, components, and services needed for a project. It involves supplier selection, purchase orders, delivery tracking, and quality inspection of received items.

Procurement Process

  • Supplier Selection – Identify and evaluate suppliers
  • Request for Quotation (RFQ) – Get competitive bids
  • Purchase Order – Formal order placement
  • Order Tracking – Monitor delivery status
  • Incoming Inspection – Quality check of materials
  • Inventory Management – Store and track items

Procurement Documents

  • Purchase Order (PO) – Contract with supplier
  • Bill of Materials (BOM) – List of required items
  • Delivery Schedule – Timeline for deliveries
  • Inspection Reports – Quality verification
  • Supplier Approval List – Approved vendors

Example: Material Inspection

Incoming material inspection includes: verifying quantity against PO, checking for damage, verifying specifications (model numbers, ratings), and documenting results. Non-conforming items are returned or replaced.
