🛒 Procurement

Procurement is the process of sourcing, purchasing, and managing the delivery of materials, equipment, and services required for an engineering project.

🔹 What is Procurement?

Procurement is the acquisition of goods, services, and equipment needed for a project. It involves identifying suppliers, obtaining quotations, placing orders, and managing delivery and quality assurance. Effective procurement ensures that materials and equipment are available on time, within budget, and meet the required specifications.

🔹 Key Steps in Procurement

  • Specification Development – Define technical requirements and quality standards
  • Supplier Selection – Identify and evaluate potential suppliers
  • Request for Quotation (RFQ) – Solicit bids from suppliers
  • Evaluation – Compare offers based on price, quality, delivery, and reputation
  • Purchase Order – Issue purchase orders with clear terms and conditions
  • Inspection & Quality Control – Verify that materials meet specifications
  • Logistics & Delivery – Manage transportation and storage

🔹 Procurement Categories

  • Materials – Cables, conduits, steel, insulation, consumables
  • Equipment – Motors, switchgear, transformers, control panels, instruments
  • Services – Engineering consulting, installation, testing, and commissioning
  • Software – Engineering software, licenses, and support

🔹 Example: Procurement for a Substation Project

A substation project requires procurement of a 132 kV transformer, switchgear, control panels, and cables. The procurement team issues RFQs to qualified suppliers, evaluates technical and commercial proposals, and awards contracts. The team conducts inspections (FAT) at the supplier's facility and manages logistics for delivery to the site.
