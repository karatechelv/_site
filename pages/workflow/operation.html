Operation Phase

System handover, operation, and ongoing maintenance after project completion.

What is Operation?

Operation is the final phase of the engineering workflow where the system is handed over to the client and put into normal use. It includes training operators, providing ongoing support, and performing routine maintenance to ensure reliable operation.

Operation Activities

  • System Handover – Formal transfer to client
  • Operator Training – Training client personnel
  • Routine Operation – Running the system normally
  • Maintenance – Preventive and corrective
  • Performance Monitoring – Tracking system performance
  • Support – Technical support and troubleshooting

Maintenance Types

  • Preventive Maintenance – Scheduled inspections and service
  • Corrective Maintenance – Fix issues as they occur
  • Predictive Maintenance – Monitoring for early warning
  • Reliability-Centered Maintenance – Focus on critical systems

Example: Ongoing Maintenance Program

An ongoing maintenance program for an MCC panel includes: monthly visual inspections, quarterly thermal imaging of connections, annual calibration of protection devices, and immediate corrective action for any faults. A maintenance log is kept for all activities.
