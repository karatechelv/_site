P&ID (Piping & Instrumentation Diagram)

Understanding and creating Piping and Instrumentation Diagrams for process systems.

What is P&ID?

A Piping and Instrumentation Diagram (P&ID) is a detailed graphical representation of a process system. It shows the interconnecting piping, equipment, instrumentation, and control devices used in a process plant. P&IDs are essential for design, operation, and maintenance.

P&ID Components

  • Piping – Process lines with sizes and materials
  • Equipment – Vessels, pumps, compressors, heat exchangers
  • Instrumentation – Sensors, transmitters, control valves
  • Control Systems – PLC/DCS connections
  • Tags and Labels – Unique identifiers
  • Flow Direction – Arrows and indications

P&ID Standards

  • ANSI/ISA 5.1 – Instrument Symbols and Diagrams
  • ISO 10628 – Flow Diagrams for Process Plants
  • API RP 551 – Process Measurement Instrumentation
  • IEC 61082 – Preparation of Documents

Example: Simple P&ID

A simple P&ID for a tank level control system shows: feed line → storage tank → level transmitter (LT) → level controller (LC) → control valve → product line. The P&ID includes tag numbers, line sizes, and control loop information.
