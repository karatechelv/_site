Assembly Phase

Panel assembly, wiring, and mechanical integration of components.

What is Assembly?

Assembly is the process of physically constructing the panel by mounting components, wiring them according to schematics, and integrating mechanical and electrical systems. It transforms the design into a functional unit ready for testing.

Assembly Steps

  • Enclosure Preparation – Mounting plates, cutouts
  • Component Mounting – PLC, breakers, relays, terminal blocks
  • Wiring – Power, control, and communication wiring
  • Busbar Installation – Power distribution
  • Labeling – Component identification
  • Visual Inspection – Check before testing

Assembly Quality Checks

  • Component Mounting – Securely fastened
  • Wiring – Correct connections and routing
  • Terminal Tightness – Proper torque
  • Labeling – Clear and correct
  • Safety – No exposed conductors

Example: Assembly Checklist

# Panel Assembly Checklist # 1. Enclosure prepared and grounded [ ] # 2. PLC and I/O modules mounted [ ] # 3. Circuit breakers installed [ ] # 4. Wiring completed per schematics [ ] # 5. Wiring ducts closed and secure [ ] # 6. All labels applied [ ] # 7. Visual inspection complete [ ]

Example: Assembly of MCC Panel

An MCC panel assembly involves mounting motor starters, PLC I/O modules, wiring power and control circuits, installing busbars, and labeling all components. The assembly is documented and inspected before moving to FAT stage.
