Panel Design Phase

Designing panel layouts, wiring, and component placement for electrical and control panels.

What is Panel Design?

Panel design is the process of creating detailed layouts and wiring diagrams for electrical and control panels. It involves arranging components, routing wiring, and ensuring compliance with safety and performance standards.

Panel Design Components

  • Power Distribution – Circuit breakers, busbars
  • Control Components – PLC, relays, contactors
  • Wiring Systems – Terminal blocks, wiring ducts
  • Protection Devices – Overload, short-circuit protection
  • HMI/Interface – Operator interface and indicators

Design Considerations

  • Space Management – Component placement and clearances
  • Heat Dissipation – Ventilation and cooling
  • IP Rating – Environmental protection
  • Safety Compliance – IEC 61439, NEC
  • Maintenance Accessibility – Ease of service

Example: Control Panel Layout

A control panel layout includes: main breaker at top, PLC rack in the center, power supplies and contactors below, wiring ducts on sides, and terminal blocks at the bottom. All components are labeled and the layout is designed for efficient wiring and maintenance.
