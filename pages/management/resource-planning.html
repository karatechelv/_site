Resource Planning

Planning and allocating human, material, and financial resources for engineering projects.

What is Resource Planning?

Resource planning is the process of identifying, allocating, and managing resources (people, materials, equipment, and budget) to ensure successful project delivery. It involves forecasting resource needs, scheduling availability, and optimizing utilization.

Types of Resources

  • Human Resources – Engineers, technicians, project managers
  • Material Resources – Components, raw materials, consumables
  • Equipment – Tools, machinery, testing equipment
  • Financial – Budget allocation and cost control
  • Facilities – Workspace, offices, warehouses

Resource Planning Process

  • Resource Identification – Define what is needed
  • Resource Estimation – Quantity and timing
  • Resource Allocation – Assign to specific tasks
  • Resource Scheduling – Timeline and availability
  • Resource Monitoring – Track and adjust

Example: Resource Optimization

Resource optimization involves balancing workload, reducing idle time, and cross-training team members. For a panel project, optimizing resources could reduce labor costs by 15% and shorten delivery time by 2 weeks.
