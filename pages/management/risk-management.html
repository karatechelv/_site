Risk Management

Identification, assessment, mitigation, and monitoring of risks in engineering projects.

What is Risk Management?

Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, and controlling threats to an organization's capital, earnings, and operations. In engineering projects, it involves systematic application of policies, procedures, and practices to minimize potential negative impacts.

Risk Management Process

  • Risk Identification – Brainstorming, checklists, FMEA
  • Risk Assessment – Likelihood and impact analysis
  • Risk Response Planning – Mitigation strategies
  • Risk Monitoring – Tracking and review
  • Risk Communication – Reporting to stakeholders

Types of Risks

  • Technical Risks – Design failures, errors
  • Schedule Risks – Delays, resource shortages
  • Cost Risks – Budget overruns
  • Quality Risks – Defects, non-conformance
  • Safety Risks – Hazards, injuries
  • Environmental Risks – Regulatory non-compliance

Risk Matrix Example

# Risk Matrix (Likelihood x Impact) # | Low Impact | Medium Impact | High Impact # Low Likelihood | Low Risk | Medium Risk | Medium Risk # Medium Likelihood | Low Risk | Medium Risk | High Risk # High Likelihood | Medium Risk | High Risk | High Risk

A risk matrix helps prioritize risks based on likelihood and impact. High probability and high impact risks require immediate action.

Example: Risk Register for Panel Project

A risk register for an MCC panel project includes risks like: design changes (medium risk), component delivery delays (high risk), assembly errors (medium risk), and testing failures (low risk). Mitigation actions are assigned to each risk.
