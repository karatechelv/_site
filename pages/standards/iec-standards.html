IEC Standards

International Electrotechnical Commission standards for electrical, electronic, and related technologies.

What is IEC?

The International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) is the world's leading organization for international standards in electrical, electronic, and related technologies. It develops standards that ensure safety, efficiency, and compatibility of electrical systems worldwide.

Key IEC Standards

  • IEC 61439 – Switchgear and controlgear assemblies
  • IEC 60204 – Electrical equipment of machines
  • IEC 60364 – Electrical installations of buildings
  • IEC 61508 – Functional safety of E/E/PE systems
  • IEC 61511 – Safety instrumented systems
  • IEC 61082 – Preparation of documents for electrotechnology
  • IEC 61131-3 – PLC programming languages
  • IEC 61784 – Industrial communication networks

IEC Standards by Category

  • Electrical Installations: IEC 60364, IEC 61439
  • Safety & Functional Safety: IEC 61508, IEC 61511
  • Automation & Control: IEC 61131-3, IEC 61784
  • Earthing & Lightning: IEC 62305
  • Energy Management: IEC 61970

Example: IEC 61439 – Switchgear

IEC 61439 specifies requirements for low-voltage switchgear and controlgear assemblies. It covers design verification, temperature rise, short-circuit withstand, and protection against electric shock. All MCC, PCC, and distribution panels must comply with this standard.
