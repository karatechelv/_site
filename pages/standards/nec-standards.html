National Electrical Code (NFPA 70) – The benchmark for safe electrical design, installation, and inspection.

What is NEC?

The National Electrical Code (NEC), published by NFPA as NFPA 70, is the most widely adopted electrical code in the United States. It provides guidelines for safe electrical installation and is used as the standard for electrical design and inspection.

Key NEC Articles

  • Article 100 – Definitions and Terms
  • Article 110 – Requirements for Electrical Installations
  • Article 200 – Use and Identification of Grounded Conductors
  • Article 210 – Branch Circuits
  • Article 230 – Services
  • Article 250 – Grounding and Bonding
  • Article 300 – Wiring Methods
  • Article 430 – Motors, Motor Circuits, and Controllers

NEC Chapters

  • Chapter 1: General Requirements
  • Chapter 2: Wiring and Protection
  • Chapter 3: Wiring Methods and Materials
  • Chapter 4: Equipment for General Use
  • Chapter 5: Special Occupancies
  • Chapter 6: Special Equipment
  • Chapter 7: Special Conditions
  • Chapter 8: Communications Systems

Example: NEC Article 250 – Grounding

Article 250 of the NEC covers grounding and bonding requirements. It specifies that all electrical systems must be grounded to provide safety against electric shock and overvoltage. Proper grounding involves connecting equipment to earth through grounding electrodes and conductors.
