ISO Standards

International Organization for Standardization standards for quality, environment, safety, and management systems.

What is ISO?

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) is an independent, non-governmental international organization that develops and publishes standards for quality, safety, efficiency, and sustainability across various industries.

Key ISO Standards

  • ISO 9001 – Quality Management Systems (QMS)
  • ISO 14001 – Environmental Management Systems
  • ISO 45001 – Occupational Health & Safety
  • ISO 50001 – Energy Management
  • ISO 17025 – Testing and Calibration Laboratories
  • ISO 19011 – Auditing Management Systems

ISO Certification Process

  • Define Quality Policy & Objectives
  • Document Procedures & Work Instructions
  • Implement the System
  • Conduct Internal Audits
  • External Audit by Certification Body
  • Obtain Certification
  • Continuous Improvement & Surveillance Audits

Example: ISO 9001 in Engineering

An engineering company implementing ISO 9001 defines its quality policy, establishes documented procedures for design, procurement, manufacturing, and testing, conducts internal audits, and obtains certification to demonstrate commitment to quality and customer satisfaction.
