IEEE Standards

Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers standards for power systems, communication, and electronics.

What is IEEE?

The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) is the world's largest technical professional organization dedicated to advancing technology. IEEE develops standards for electrical, electronic, and computer systems that are globally recognized.

Key IEEE Standards

  • IEEE 80 – Earthing and Grounding
  • IEEE 141 – Electric Power Distribution
  • IEEE 242 – Protection and Coordination
  • IEEE 315 – Graphic Symbols for Electrical Diagrams
  • IEEE 519 – Harmonic Control in Power Systems
  • IEEE 1584 – Arc Flash Hazard Calculations
  • IEEE 802.3 – Ethernet Standard

IEEE Standards by Category

  • Power Systems: IEEE 80, IEEE 141, IEEE 519
  • Protection & Coordination: IEEE 242, IEEE 1584
  • Communication: IEEE 802.3 (Ethernet)
  • Symbols & Documentation: IEEE 315
  • Electronics & Semiconductors: IEEE 1241, IEEE 1451

Example: IEEE 80 – Earthing

IEEE 80 provides guidelines for safe earthing and grounding systems in substations and industrial facilities. It covers step and touch voltage calculations, soil resistivity measurement, and grounding grid design to ensure personnel safety during fault conditions.
