Overview of HSE

A comprehensive overview of Health, Safety, and Environment engineering for industrial projects.

What is HSE?

HSE stands for Health, Safety, and Environment. It is a multidisciplinary field focused on protecting people, property, and the environment from hazards in the workplace. HSE engineering involves identifying risks, implementing control measures, and ensuring regulatory compliance.

Key HSE Areas

  • Safety – Hazard identification, risk assessment, and control
  • Health – Occupational health, hygiene, and wellness
  • Environment – Pollution control, waste management, sustainability
  • Emergency Response – Preparedness and crisis management
  • Compliance – Regulatory and legal requirements

HSE Standards

  • ISO 45001 – Occupational Health & Safety Management
  • ISO 14001 – Environmental Management Systems
  • OSHA – Occupational Safety and Health Administration
  • EPA – Environmental Protection Agency regulations
