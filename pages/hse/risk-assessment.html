Risk Assessment

Systematic hazard identification, risk analysis, and control measures for workplace safety.

What is Risk Assessment?

Risk assessment is the process of identifying hazards, analyzing risks, and determining appropriate control measures. It is a key component of HSE management to prevent accidents and protect people and the environment.

Risk Assessment Steps

  • Hazard Identification вЂ“ Identify all potential hazards
  • Risk Analysis вЂ“ Determine likelihood and severity
  • Risk Evaluation вЂ“ Prioritize risks for action
  • Risk Control вЂ“ Select and implement controls
  • Review and Monitor вЂ“ Periodic reassessment

Risk Assessment Methods

  • JSA (Job Safety Analysis) вЂ“ Analyze individual tasks
  • HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study) вЂ“ Process safety
  • FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis) вЂ“ Equipment reliability
  • Bow-Tie Analysis вЂ“ Consequence and prevention
  • What-If Analysis вЂ“ Scenario-based risk assessment

Risk Matrix Example

# Risk Matrix (Likelihood vs Severity) # Severity: Low, Medium, High, Very High # Likelihood: Rare, Unlikely, Possible, Likely, Almost Certain # Classification: # - Green: Low Risk (Acceptable) # - Yellow: Medium Risk (Requires attention) # - Red: High Risk (Requires immediate action)

A risk matrix helps categorize risks and prioritize actions.

Example: Risk Assessment for Panel Testing

A risk assessment for panel testing includes: electrical shock (high risk), arc flash (high risk), heavy lifting (medium risk), and noise (medium risk). Control measures include: LOTO procedures, PPE, proper lifting equipment, and noise monitoring.
