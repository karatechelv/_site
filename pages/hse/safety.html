Safety Engineering

Hazard identification, risk assessment, safety controls, and workplace safety management.

What is Safety Engineering?

Safety engineering is the discipline that deals with the protection of people, property, and the environment from hazards. It involves identifying potential hazards, assessing risks, and implementing controls to prevent accidents and injuries.

Hazard Types

  • Physical Hazards – Noise, vibration, temperature extremes
  • Chemical Hazards – Toxic, flammable, corrosive substances
  • Electrical Hazards – Shock, arc flash, fire
  • Mechanical Hazards – Moving parts, crushing, shearing
  • Ergonomic Hazards – Repetitive strain, lifting, posture
  • Psychosocial Hazards – Stress, fatigue, work overload

Safety Controls Hierarchy

  • Elimination – Remove the hazard entirely
  • Substitution – Replace with safer alternative
  • Engineering Controls – Guarding, ventilation, isolation
  • Administrative Controls – Procedures, training, signage
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) – Hard hats, gloves, glasses

Lock Out Tag Out (LOTO) Procedure

# LOTO Procedure for Electrical Panel # 1. Identify all energy sources # 2. Notify affected personnel # 3. Shut down equipment # 4. Isolate energy sources # 5. Apply lock and tag # 6. Verify isolation (try/test) # 7. Perform work # 8. Remove lock and tag # 9. Re-energize and notify

Example: Arc Flash Safety

Arc flash is a dangerous electrical hazard. Safety measures include proper PPE (arc-rated clothing, face shield), maintaining safe distances, using insulated tools, and following NFPA 70E guidelines. An arc flash risk assessment determines the incident energy level.
