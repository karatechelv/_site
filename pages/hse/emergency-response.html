Emergency Response

Emergency preparedness, response plans, and crisis management for engineering facilities.

What is Emergency Response?

Emergency response involves preparing for, responding to, and recovering from emergency situations such as fires, chemical spills, medical emergencies, and natural disasters. It aims to protect life, property, and the environment.

Types of Emergencies

  • Fire – Electrical fires, chemical fires
  • Chemical Spills – Hazardous material release
  • Medical Emergencies – Injuries, heart attacks
  • Electrical Accidents – Shock, arc flash
  • Natural Disasters – Earthquakes, floods, storms
  • Security Incidents – Break-ins, threats

Emergency Response Plan (ERP)

  • Emergency procedures – Step-by-step actions
  • Evacuation routes and assembly points
  • Emergency contacts and communication
  • First aid and medical response
  • Fire suppression and spill containment
  • Training and drills

Emergency Response Example

# Fire Emergency Procedure # 1. Raise alarm (activate fire alarm) # 2. Call emergency services (119/911) # 3. Evacuate via nearest safe exit # 4. Assemble at designated point # 5. Do not use elevators # 6. Do not re-enter until authorized

Example: Chemical Spill Response

A chemical spill response includes: evacuate area, alert others, isolate the spill using spill kit, neutralize if possible, contain and clean up using appropriate PPE, and dispose of waste according to regulations. Report the incident and review procedures.
