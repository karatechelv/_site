Occupational Health

Workplace health, hygiene, ergonomics, and wellness in engineering environments.

What is Occupational Health?

Occupational health is the branch of healthcare focused on the physical and mental well-being of workers. It involves preventing work-related injuries and illnesses, promoting health, and ensuring a safe and healthy work environment.

Health Hazards

  • Chemical Exposure – Gases, vapors, dusts, fumes
  • Biological Hazards – Bacteria, viruses, fungi
  • Physical Agents – Noise, heat, cold, vibration
  • Ergonomic Factors – Repetitive tasks, heavy lifting
  • Psychological Factors – Stress, burnout, bullying

Occupational Health Programs

  • Medical Surveillance – Health monitoring and screening
  • Health Promotion – Wellness programs, fitness, nutrition
  • Stress Management – Counseling, mental health support
  • Ergonomic Assessments – Workstation optimization
  • Hearing Conservation – Noise monitoring and protection

Ergonomics in Engineering

  • Workstation Setup – Proper chair height, monitor position
  • Manual Handling – Safe lifting techniques
  • Repetitive Strain Prevention – Regular breaks, stretching
  • Tool Design – Ergonomic tools reduce strain

Good ergonomics reduces injuries and improves productivity.

Example: Hearing Conservation Program

A hearing conservation program includes: noise monitoring to identify hazardous areas, providing hearing protection (earplugs or earmuffs), training on hearing protection, and regular audiometric testing to detect early hearing loss.
