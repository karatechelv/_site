Control Valves

Control valves, actuators, positioners, and their applications in industrial process control.

What are Control Valves?

Control valves are final control elements that regulate fluid flow by varying the size of the flow passage. They are operated by actuators that receive signals from controllers (PLC/DCS) to adjust valve position based on process requirements.

Types of Control Valves

  • Globe Valves – Most common, good throttling
  • Ball Valves – Quarter-turn, on/off or throttling
  • Butterfly Valves – Lightweight, large diameter
  • Gate Valves – On/off, not for throttling
  • Diaphragm Valves – For corrosive fluids
  • Pinch Valves – For slurries and abrasive fluids

Actuators

  • Pneumatic Actuators – Most common (3-15 psi, 4-20mA)
  • Electric Actuators – High accuracy, no air supply needed
  • Hydraulic Actuators – High force, heavy-duty applications
  • Manual Actuators – Handwheel for backup

Positioners

  • Pneumatic Positioners – Analog, simple
  • Electro-Pneumatic Positioners – Digital control
  • Smart Positioners – HART/Fieldbus, diagnostics

Positioners ensure accurate valve positioning by comparing the valve stem position with the control signal and making corrections.

Example: Control Valve Sizing

A control valve is sized using the Cv (flow coefficient) value. For a given flow rate and pressure drop, the required Cv is calculated. Example: For water flow of 100 GPM with 10 psi pressure drop, Cv = 100 / √10 ≈ 31.6.
