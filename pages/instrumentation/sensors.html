Sensors in Instrumentation

Industrial sensors for measuring temperature, pressure, flow, level, and other process variables.

What are Industrial Sensors?

Industrial sensors are devices that detect and respond to changes in physical conditions such as temperature, pressure, flow, or level. They convert these physical parameters into electrical signals that can be read by control systems.

Temperature Sensors

  • Thermocouples (Type K, J, T, etc.)
  • Resistance Temperature Detectors (RTD – PT100)
  • Thermistors (NTC, PTC)
  • Infrared / Pyrometers (Non-contact)
  • Temperature Transmitters (4-20mA, HART)

Pressure Sensors

  • Piezoelectric Pressure Sensors
  • Strain Gauge Pressure Transducers
  • Capacitive Pressure Sensors
  • Differential Pressure Transmitters
  • Absolute, Gauge, and Vacuum Sensors

Flow & Level Sensors

  • Magnetic Flow Meters (Magflow)
  • Coriolis Mass Flow Meters
  • Ultrasonic Flow Meters
  • Radar Level Sensors
  • Ultrasonic Level Sensors
  • Capacitance Level Switches

Example: Temperature Measurement System

A PT100 RTD sensor measures temperature in a chemical reactor. The sensor sends a resistance signal to a temperature transmitter, which converts it to a 4-20mA signal and sends it to the PLC/DCS for control and monitoring.
