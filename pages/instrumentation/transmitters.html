Transmitters in Instrumentation

Industrial transmitters for converting sensor signals to standardized outputs (4-20mA, HART, Foundation Fieldbus).

What are Transmitters?

Transmitters are devices that convert the signal from a sensor into a standardized output signal (typically 4-20mA, 0-10V, or digital protocols like HART, Foundation Fieldbus, or Profibus PA) for transmission to control systems.

Types of Transmitters

  • Temperature Transmitters – RTD, Thermocouple
  • Pressure Transmitters – Absolute, Gauge, Differential
  • Flow Transmitters – Magnetic, Coriolis, Ultrasonic
  • Level Transmitters – Radar, Ultrasonic, Hydrostatic
  • Analytical Transmitters – pH, Conductivity, Oxygen

Communication Protocols

  • 4-20mA (Analog) – Most common, simple and reliable
  • HART (Highway Addressable Remote Transducer) – Digital over 4-20mA
  • Foundation Fieldbus – Digital, two-way communication
  • Profibus PA – Process automation fieldbus
  • WirelessHART – Wireless communication

Example: 4-20mA Loop Calculation

// Pressure Transmitter: 0-100 bar = 4-20mA // Formula: I_out = 4 + (PV / Range) * 16 // For PV = 75 bar: I_out = 4 + (75/100) * 16 = 4 + 12 = 16 mA

A pressure transmitter with range 0-100 bar outputs 16 mA at 75 bar.

Smart Transmitters

Modern smart transmitters include microprocessors for diagnostic functions, self-calibration, digital communication, and remote configuration. Examples: Rosemount 3051, Siemens SITRANS, Yokogawa EJA.
