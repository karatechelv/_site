Analyzers in Instrumentation

Process analyzers for measuring chemical and physical properties including pH, conductivity, oxygen, and gas composition.

What are Process Analyzers?

Process analyzers are instruments that continuously measure the chemical or physical properties of a process stream. They provide real-time data for process control, quality assurance, and safety monitoring.

Types of Analyzers

  • pH Analyzers – Measure acidity/alkalinity
  • Conductivity Analyzers – Measure ion concentration
  • Oxygen Analyzers – Measure dissolved oxygen (DO) or gas O₂
  • Gas Analyzers – NDIR, paramagnetic, electrochemical
  • Colorimeters – Measure color intensity
  • Turbidity Analyzers – Measure suspended particles

Gas Analysis Techniques

  • NDIR (Non-Dispersive Infrared) – CO₂, CO, CH₄
  • Paramagnetic – O₂ analysis
  • Electrochemical – Toxic gases (H₂S, CO, NO₂)
  • GC (Gas Chromatography) – Complex gas mixtures
  • Mass Spectrometry – High-precision analysis

Liquid Analysis Parameters

  • pH – 0 to 14 scale, important in chemical processes
  • Conductivity – Indicates dissolved ions
  • Dissolved Oxygen – Critical in water treatment
  • ORP (Oxidation-Reduction Potential) – Disinfection control
  • Turbidity – Water clarity measurement

Example: pH Control in a Chemical Reactor

A pH analyzer continuously measures the pH of the reactor contents. The signal is sent to a controller that adjusts the dosing pump for acid or base to maintain the setpoint (e.g., pH 7.0 ± 0.2). This ensures product quality and process stability.
